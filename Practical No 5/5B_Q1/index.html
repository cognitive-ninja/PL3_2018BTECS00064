First Let’s Get The Basics Right — Quantum Computers Are Not Like Our Traditional Computers

Quantum computers are more powerful than our traditional computers. However we can’t add more power to the traditional computers to make them into the quantum computers.

Turning a traditional computer into a quantum computer is like making a diesel car out of an electric car. Just like we can’t add more electrical engines into an electrical car to convert it into a diesel car, we cannot provide more power to a traditional computer to make it into a quantum computer.

Our traditional computers cannot solve the problems that the quantum computers can solve. This is because quantum computers are different.

Our traditional computers transmit data in binary format. Each character that you are seeing on this article can be written in binary format. The information that the computer transmits is a stream of electrical pulses which are known as bits. A bit is in one of the two states: either 0 or 1.

Quantum computers bits are known as Qubits, which can be in multiple states simultaneously.
Qubit can be any state between 0 to 1

This very property of quantum computers make them different than our traditional computers. The property is known as superposition.
Superposition is when a Qubit can be in multiple states at the same time

Subsequently, if you were playing the game with a quantum computer then the quantum computer will always place itself in a superposition which will mean it is On and Off at the same time. Just before the results are tested, they would switch themselves into the winning state.
When the qubits are finally measured, they set themselves to one state and emerge as either being in state of 0 or 1

Imagine you dilute orange juice in water. And then you build a machine to extract the juice out of the water. The diluted state is how Qubits are.

This article explained the concept of quantum computing in an easy to understand manner. It also outlined the key differences between our traditional computers and super smart quantum computers.

IBM, Google, Rigetti, IonQ and also a large number of startups are investing into Quantum Computing. These are exciting advancements in our technological world.
